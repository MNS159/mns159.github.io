The move responds to international demands for governance reforms, particularly from EU and Arab donors who tie financial support to democratic renewal.

However, skepticism remains after past cancellations, and turnout may reflect disillusionment. International actors view this as a test for post-war governance. The U.S.-brokered ceasefire envisions a technocratic Gaza administration, but elections could complicate power-sharing. This case highlights the growing tension between AI companies and content creators over the use of copyrighted material in training AI models. OpenAI denies the allegations, framing the lawsuit as an invasion of user privacy and defending its adherence to fair use principles.

VERAXA Biotech advances T-cell therapy with ATUM partnership

VERAXA Biotech has partnered with ATUM to develop cell lines for its lead BiTAC-TCE cancer therapy, moving closer to clinical trials. The collaboration leverages ATUM's proprietary gene insertion technology to streamline manufacturing of VERAXA's dual-targeting T-cell engager, which aims to reduce off-tumor toxicity while maintaining efficacy.

VERAXA Biotech (NASDAQ: VRXA) has taken a critical step toward clinical development of its lead cancer therapy by selecting ATUM to generate stable cell lines for manufacturing. The partnership will use ATUM's Leap-In Transposase® technology, which has supported over 50 investigational new drug (IND) applications, to produce the complex dual-component BiTAC-TCE therapy.

The BiTAC platform represents a novel approach to T-cell engagers, which typically face challenges with toxicity when their target antigens appear on healthy cells. VERAXA's therapy splits the engager into two inactive precursors that only activate when both bind to distinct markers on the same cancer cell—a design that showed promising tumor selectivity in preclinical studies presented at AACR 2026.

Cell line development marks a transition from research to potential clinical use, addressing manufacturing needs for future trials. The ATUM collaboration focuses on creating stable, high-producing cell lines capable of consistently generating the therapy's two-component structure—a technical hurdle for multi-chain biologics.

VERAXA's progress comes amid renewed investor interest in biotech, particularly cancer immunotherapies. The company recently expanded its Heidelberg R&D facilities by 60%, signaling commitment to advancing its pipeline. However, the BiTAC platform's clinical potential remains unproven, with IND-enabling studies still ahead.

The partnership reflects growing industry reliance on specialized CDMOs for complex therapies. ATUM's transposase technology offers potential advantages over traditional random integration methods for gene insertion, particularly for therapies requiring balanced expression of multiple protein chains like VERAXA's dual-targeting engager.
